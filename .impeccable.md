## Design Context

### Users
Developer-led teams, indie founders, and small SaaS builders use feedbacks.dev to install feedback collection quickly, trust the snippet immediately, and triage product signal without wading through enterprise clutter. Public surfaces should help those same teams feel proud to share roadmap and feedback discussions in public, while keeping the interaction simple enough for end users to vote and post without friction.

### Brand Personality
Developer-first, trustworthy, and sharp. The product should feel calm, precise, and mature rather than playful, bubbly, or over-designed. It should communicate operational confidence and product seriousness without becoming cold or corporate.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use the dashboard as the source of truth for tone: compact information hierarchy, crisp borders, restrained accent usage, and stronger contrast. Public surfaces should feel more editorial and product-grade than marketing-sweet. Avoid oversized radii, pill overload, washed-out gradients, and toy-like voting UI. Default to a light-first presentation with neutral structure and a controlled green accent.

### Design Principles
1. Match the dashboard language before adding any new flourish.
2. Sharper structure beats softer decoration on public boards.
3. Strong contrast and information rhythm should make scanning effortless.
4. Navigation between boards should feel intentional and curated, not like an endless pile of generic cards.
5. Voting, status, and replies should feel credible enough for real product decisions, not like casual social reactions.
